DISI Global Oaxaca Fellow Application

Diverse Intelligences Summer Institute — Global Edition, Oaxaca, México

DISI Global Oaxaca is seeking researchers and graduate students in fields including — but not limited to — biology, anthropology, linguistics, ethnobiology, computer science, agroecology, psychology, philosophy, sociology, the arts, and activism, who are interested in pursuing interdisciplinary, collaborative research on diverse intelligences, broadly construed, to join us in Oaxaca, México, as DISI Global Fellows during February–March 2027 (exact dates TBA).

DISI Global Fellows will be hosted by the organizing team within the Research Institute on Applied Mathematics and Systems (IIMAS), National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), for an immersive week in Oaxaca. Fellows will take part in a program shaped collectively by the community — including talks, workshops, discussion tables, guided activities, and a shared collective research project and storytelling project.

In addition to participating as fellows, many participants will be invited to serve as presenters, facilitators, or instructors for sessions and activities, so that the DISI Global Oaxaca community can learn from the expertise and perspectives they bring.

Mentorship

Mentors & hosts

All DISI Global Fellows will be assigned a mentor (for graduate students, postdocs, and early-career researchers) or a host (for senior faculty), ensuring every participant has a primary point of contact. Junior fellows will have the opportunity for explicit mentorship, organized around one or more goals established in conversation with their mentor.

Funding

Travel support

Limited funds are available to help cover travel and participation costs for DISI Global Fellows. Details will be shared with accepted applicants.

Eligibility

Who can apply

Graduate students

With interest in fields including biology, anthropology, linguistics, ethnobiology, computer science, agroecology, psychology, philosophy, sociology, the arts, or related areas.

Early-career researchers

Pursuing interdisciplinary and collaborative research on diverse intelligences, broadly construed.

Faculty & practitioners

Senior faculty, activists, and storytellers conducting interdisciplinary work related to any of the above fields.
