About the event

Ecological and situated intelligence, explored in community

DISI Global Oaxaca, México.

Introduction

WDISI GLobal Oaxaca aimg to bring the DISI experience to Oaxaca, in southeastern Mexico, a region with a rich biological and cultural diversity. In doing so, we aim to situate the DISI Global Oaxaca experience in a local to explore in community ecological and situated intelligence.

Purpose and objectives

A central question

How does an intelligent relationship between communities and their environment emerge and evolve within specific ecological and cultural contexts?

The cultural and biological diversity in Oaxaca, as well as the relationships local communities have developed with their environment, provide a powerful setting for exploring this question. This DISI Global event will explore the collective dimension of ecological and situated intelligence across biological, social, and artificial systems, creating an immersive DISI experience that highlights diversity and collective action as central to intelligent relationships between communities and their environment.

Grounded in the long-standing ecological practices of Oaxacan communities, participants will examine how community organization, collective action, linguistic diversity, and agroecological knowledge give rise to embodied, adaptive forms of ecological and situated intelligence that have sustained collective, adaptive relationships with the local environment — and how these insights can inform the design of context-sensitive and culturally responsive artificial systems and related policy frameworks.

Bringing together early-career scholars, senior faculty, activists, and storytellers from biology, anthropology, linguistics, ethnobiology, computer science, agroecology, psychology, the arts, and beyond, the program will be designed to create a DISI community committed to dialogue, experimentation, and intellectual risk-taking.

The main aim of this DISI Global event is to co-develop new conceptual and practical frameworks for understanding ecological and situated intelligence, while critically reflecting on their broader societal implications — including the potential for collective, adaptive relationships with local environments, and the replicability of these strategies in other regions of the world with different social and ecological conditions.

What makes this DISI special

Five commitments that shape the experience

1

Collective intelligence in practice

Collective and situated intelligence will be reflected in every aspect of the event, learning, creating, and proposing as a community, not as an audience.

2

Space for uncertainty

The event offers a space to share and embrace uncertainty, and to explore, together, possible ways of navigating it.

3

Situated, together and apart

We reflect on what it means to be situated in a shared physical context, while recognizing that distance doesn't mean we stop being situated. Rather, we are situated within a broader context. That makes room for different perspectives on the same problem, on things that matter to all of us and help us make sense together.

4

The pillars of the DISI community

Building bonds, identities, and meanings; breaking down the barrier of the unfamiliar.

5

Aligned interests, generously shared

We look for strategies to identify shared interests. The local community holds a clarity that comes from lived experience, their life, their appreciation of life, and their persistence depend on it. That clarity generates a disposition to listen, and makes it easier to align with what genuinely interests you.

Creation and organization

Built by everyone who participates

  • There will be a single research project and a single storytelling project. Both will be developed collectively, involving all participants.
  • Everyone takes part in organizing DISI — the talks and events that take place, as well as the development of the project and whatever is built together as storytellers.
  • In their application, applicants should not only state their interests and project ideas, but also describe what they can contribute to DISI. This contribution need not be a talk, it can be a reflection, a discussion table, a guided activity, a workshop, or any format that contributes to collective exploration: conceptual, methodological, technical, or creative.
  • Personalized invitations will be extended to people who could take part as speakers or storytellers, indicating what we believe they could contribute to the collective creation, while leaving the door open for them to propose something different.
  • Local community members are warmly invited to be parto of DISI Global OAxaca. Participation can be on their own terms.
