Why Oaxaca

Situating DISI in a new ecological and cultural context

We aim to recreate the experience of DISI fellows and faculty by bringing it to Oaxaca — a region of extraordinary biological and cultural diversity — situating the DISI Global experience in a local context that differs greatly from Scotland, while sharing the same values and practices that define the DISI community.

Grounded in the long-standing ecological practices of Oaxacan communities, participants will examine how community organization, collective action, linguistic diversity, and agroecological knowledge give rise to embodied, adaptive forms of ecological and situated intelligence — and how these insights can inform context-sensitive, culturally responsive artificial systems and policy frameworks.

A community, not an audience

Built collectively, from the first invitation

There is a single research project and a single storytelling project — both developed collectively, involving every participant. Everyone takes part in organizing DISI, in its talks and events, and in whatever is built together.

Applicants are asked not only to share their interests, but what they can contribute: a talk, a reflection, a discussion table, a guided activity, a workshop — any format that supports collective, conceptual, methodological, technical, or creative exploration.
